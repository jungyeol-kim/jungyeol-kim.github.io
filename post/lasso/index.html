Lasso and Estimation Bound

Dec 1, 2020 2 min read R

One of the types of errors that we are concerned with in Lasso regression is the prediction loss: $ ||X (\hat{\beta}-\beta^{\star})||^2_2. $ For the model $ Y=X\beta^{\star}+\epsilon $, where $ \epsilon \sim \text{subG}(\sigma^2) $, the Lasso solution is

$$ \hat{\beta} := \hat{\beta}_{Lasso} \in argmin_{\beta} \frac{1}{2n} ||Y-X\beta||^2 +\lambda_n ||\beta||_1. $$ Theorem: Let A be the event {$ \lambda_n \geq n^{-1} ||X^T \epsilon ||_{\infty} $}. If A holds, then

$$ MSE(\hat{\beta}) = \frac{||X (\hat{\beta}-\beta^{\star})||^2}{n} \leq 4 ||\beta^{\star}||_1 \lambda_n $$

Proof: By the definition of Lasso, we have

\begin{align} \frac{1}{2n} ||Y-X\hat{\beta}||_2^2 + \lambda_n ||\hat{\beta}||_1 \leq \frac{1}{2n} ||Y-X{\beta}^{\star}||^2 + \lambda_n ||{\beta}^{\star}||_1 \end{align}

We use true model equation $ Y=X{\beta}^{\star}+\epsilon $. By substituting $ Y $ for $ X{\beta}^{\star}+\epsilon $, we have

\begin{align} \frac{1}{2n} ||X (\hat{\beta}-\beta^{\star})||_2^2 & \leq \frac{\epsilon^\top X (\hat{\beta}-\beta^{\star})}{n} + \lambda_n (||\beta^{\star}||_1-||\hat{\beta}||_1)\\
& (\text{by Holder’s Inequality, }\epsilon^\top X (\hat{\beta}-\beta^{\star}) = |\sum_{i=1}^{d}(\epsilon^\top X)_i (\hat{\beta}-\beta^{\star})_i| \leq ||\epsilon^\top X||_{\infty} ||\hat{\beta}-\beta^{\star}||_1) \nonumber \\ & \leq \frac{||\epsilon^\top X||_{\infty} ||\hat{\beta}-\beta^{\star}||_1}{n} + \lambda_n(||\beta^{\star}||_1-||\hat{\beta}||_1)\\
& (\text{by Triangle Inequality, }||\hat{\beta}-\beta^{\star}||_1 \leq ||\hat{\beta}||_1 + ||-\beta^{\star}||_1 = ||\hat{\beta}||_1 + ||\beta^{\star}||_1) \nonumber \\
& \leq \frac{||X^\top \epsilon||_{\infty}}{n} (||\hat{\beta}||_1 + ||\beta^{\star}||_1) + \lambda_n(||\beta^{\star}||_1-||\hat{\beta}||_1)\\
& = ||\hat{\beta}||_1 {\underbrace{(\frac{||X^\top \epsilon||_{\infty}}{n} - \lambda_n)}_{\leq 0}} + ||{\beta}^{\star}||_1 {\underbrace{(\frac{||X^\top \epsilon||_{\infty}}{n} + \lambda_n)}_{\leq 2\lambda_n}} \\
& (\because \frac{||X^\top \epsilon||_{\infty}}{n} - \lambda_n \leq 0 \text{ by the condition of the Theorem 1}) \nonumber\\
& \leq ||\beta^{\star}||_1 \cdot 2\lambda_n \label{eqn:2} \end{align}

So we see the last expression $ \frac{1}{n} ||X (\hat{\beta}-\beta^{\star})||_2^2 \leq 4 ||\beta^{\star}||_1 \lambda_n $. This finishes the proof.
